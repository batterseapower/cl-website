Papers

Types are calling conventions [Discuss]

with Simon Peyton Jones, May 2009. Submitted to the Haskell Symposium '09.

Describes a nice little intermediate language for compiling a lazy functional programming language, and some optimisations we can express with it.

Research Interests

  • Extending the capabilities of functional programming languages. I have contributed various things to the Glasgow Haskell Compiler (GHC), including SQL-like list comprehensions and compiler plugins support (as part of the Google Summer of Code).
  • Optimization of functional programs. Again, I've done most of my work in this area in the context of GHC. Nothing publishable yet, but I have some things on the go.
  • Hybrid strict/non-strict evaluation orders in functional programming languages. My paper (with Simon Peyton Jones) on types as calling conventions is the first step to providing the ability for users of Haskell to write programs that incorporate strict values in a natural and comprehensible way.
